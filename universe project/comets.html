COMETS
  • A comet is an icy body that releases gas or dust.
  • Comets travel around the sun in long, oval orbits.
  • A comet consists of a solid nucleus (core) surrounded by a cloudy atmosphere called the coma and one or two tails.
  • Astronomers classify comets according to how long they take to orbit the sun.
  • Short-period comets need less than 200 years to complete one orbit, while long-period comets take 200 years or longer.
  • Halley's comet which is visible from earth once in 76 years, was seen in about 8BC, and so some say that it was the bible's star of bethlehem.
