JUPITER'S MOONS
  • On Jan. 7, 1610, Galileo Galilei's improvements to the telescope enabled humanity to see Jupiter's four largest moons for the first time.
  • Io, Europa, Ganymede and Callisto--the so-called Galilean satellites--were seen by the Long Range Reconnaissance Imager on the New Horizons spacecraft during its flyby of Jupiter in late February 2007.
  • Io is notable for its active volcanism, which New Horizons studied extensively.
  • On the other hand, Europa's smooth, icy surface likely conceals an ocean of liquid water.
  • Ganymede, the largest moon in the solar system, has a dirty ice surface cut by fractures and peppered by impact craters.
